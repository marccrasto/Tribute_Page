Dire Straits

Mixed with blues and a dash of country-the Dire Straits were one of the few rock bands to break out in an era dominated by punk and disco.

Here's a timeline of the Dire Straits:

  • 1977 - Dire Straits formed in London by Mark Knopfler, David Knopfler, John Illsley, and Pick Withers.
  • 1978 - Gained recognition with their hit single "Sultans of Swing" from their self-titled debut album, "Dire Straits."
  • 1980 - Released "Making Movies," featuring the hit "Romeo and Juliet." David Knopfler left the band during the recording and was replaced by Hal Lindes.
  • 1982 - Released "Love Over Gold," which included the epic track "Telegraph Road." It was marked by a more progressive rock sound.
  • 1983 - Released the live album "Alchemy: Dire Straits Live," showcasing their live performance prowess.
  • 1985 - Achieved massive success with "Brothers in Arms," featuring hits like "Money for Nothing," "Walk of Life," and "So Far Away." The album won multiple awards and sold over 30 million copies worldwide.
  • 1986 - Won Grammy Award for Best Rock Performance by a Duo or Group with Vocal for "Money for Nothing." Dire Straits also became one of the first bands to release a CD.
  • 1988 - The band members took a break from recording and touring, leading to rumors of a breakup.
  • 1991 - Returned with their final studio album, "On Every Street," followed by a world tour. This tour was commercially successful but marked by internal band tensions.
  • 1995 - Dire Straits disbanded after their final tour. Mark Knopfler focused on his solo career.
  • 2018 - Inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame. Mark Knopfler chose not to attend the ceremony, highlighting ongoing tensions and the finality of the band's disbandment.
